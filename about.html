About Me

Howdy!

My name is Shawn Tan, and I'm currently an junior undergraduate computer science student. This means that my life is esentially composed of programming assignments and internship applications at the moment (I'm only partially joking). However, when I have free time, I have a variety of activities I choose to partake in. Some of my favorite hobbies include playing competitive tennis, jump-roping, gaming, and sleeping.

Tennis

When I was in high school, the majority of my free time as spent on the tennis court. I was a varsity player for The Liberal Arts and Science Academy and also participated in USTA tournaments for state-ranking points. At one point in time, I was ranked 85th in Texas and was traveling at least once a month to participate in tournaments, including SCMZs and the annual Texas Slam. Originally, my goal was to play on a competitive college team, but ultimately, I decided to get a degree in computer science instead. Today, I still enjoy playing tennis and am an active member of the A&M Recreational Tennis Team, through which I still enter tournaments and compete against other universities.

Jump Roping

Contrary to popular belief, jumping rope is not an activity exclusive to elementary school girls (I swear). It's great for cardio, and can be done anywhere for a quick workout. After entering college, playing tennis all the time wasn't really an option, so I used jump-rope exercises as an effective substitute. I have fun performing tricks, and the accessibility/affordability of the jump rope makes it an easy way to stay in shape while in school. Also, I hate treadmills.

Gaming

More of a guilty pleasure these days, video games have been one of the biggest time-consumers for me in the past. Although I don't get many chances to play them anymore, the types of video games I enjoy the most are competitive "player vs. player" games (such as Super Smash Bros. Ultimate or Counter-Strike), and almost anything from Nintendo.

Sleeping

I don't get enough and I need more.
